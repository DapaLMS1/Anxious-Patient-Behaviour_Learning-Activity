Anxious Patient Scenarios

Instructions

Watch the video and consider how you would respond in each of the scenarios. If you are not sure, try the 'Get a tip'. Once you have considered how you respond, Reveal the Response.

Scenario 1

Panic & Sensory Overload

Patient: Male, 30s.
Behavior: Hand on forehead, rapid speech, looks trapped.
Quote: "I don't think I can do this right now. I'm feeling incredibly anxious about the drill."

Establish Control: Anxiety often comes from feeling trapped. Give the patient a physical "stop signal" so they know they are in charge of the pace.
"I can see that you are feeling really overwhelmed right now. That is okay. We are going to put you in control today.

If you raise your left hand like this [demonstrate], we will stop immediately. We won't proceed until you are ready. Let's take a moment to breathe first."
Scenario 2

Avoidance & Rescheduling

Patient: Female, 20s.
Behavior: Clutching napkin, pleading tone.
Quote: "I really don't want the needle... Maybe we can just reschedule?"

Validation vs. Avoidance: Validate her feelings so she feels heard, but kindly remind her that rescheduling often leads to more pain later. Focus on immediate comfort (numbing gel).
"I hear you, and it is completely normal to feel nervous about the needle.

However, if we delay this, your toothache is likely to get much worse. We can apply some extra numbing gel first so you won't feel the pinch at all. Let's just try the gel and see how you feel?"
Scenario 3

Doubt & Recovery

Patient: Male, 60s.
Behavior: Skeptical, defensive body language.
Quote: "Are you sure this is the only option? I'm really not keen on the recovery time."

Scope of Practice: Acknowledge his concern about recovery. Do not try to defend the diagnosis yourself. Bring the dentist back to explain the "Why" to build trust.
"I completely understand why you'd want to minimize recovery time. Nobody wants to be out of action.

Since you have questions about other options, the best thing is for me to ask the Doctor to pop back in. They can explain exactly why they recommended this specific plan for your long-term health."
